══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
WINNER: H6 — SOBEL EDGE PREPROCESSING
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Sobel edge map preprocessing delivers edge_r = 0.0508 vs baseline 0.0038.
Delta: +0.047 (exceeds +0.01 threshold by 4.7x).

Implementation:
    from skimage.filters import sobel
    edges = sobel(gray_float)   # Sobel magnitude
    edges = edges / edges.max() # normalize to [0,1]
    save as PNG for Julia

Why it works:
    The SOR solver encodes GRADIENTS. A bright region in the input → gentle
    lens surface → diffuse spread → dim caustic. The caustic output IS
    fundamentally an edge/gradient image. By feeding the solver an explicit
    edge map, we directly align the solver's input distribution with what it
    naturally produces, removing one level of indirection. The solver gets
    to concentrate light exactly where the edges are, rather than discovering
    edges from a gradient image.

══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
SECOND BEST: H1H3 — LINEARIZE + INVERT
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Gamma linearization followed by inversion: edge_r = 0.0145, delta = +0.011.
Just meets the +0.01 confirmed-improvement threshold.

Why inversion helps: Inkbrush.png is predominantly light (mean brightness
157.9/255). Most of the image is background white. When inverted, dark ink
strokes become bright (high energy regions), concentrating light into the
strokes rather than the background. This aligns the transport direction with
the physical intuition: "bright regions in input → bright caustic".

Why linearization helps when combined with inversion: The sRGB gamma curve
compresses dark regions. Linearizing expands the mid-tones, giving the dark
ink strokes more contrast range after inversion, which the solver can exploit
for sharper transport gradients.

Why linearization alone HURTS: Without inversion, linearizing darkens the
overall image (the bright background stays bright, but mid-tones shift down),
reducing the effective energy gradient the solver sees.

══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
WHAT DOES NOT WORK
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Unsharp mask (H4a/b/c): HURTS performance, more at higher strength.
    The solver interprets high-frequency sharpening artifacts as structured
    gradients and tries to encode them into the lens surface, degrading the
    transport quality of the real content.

Binary threshold (H5): HURTS performance (delta -0.007).
    Removing gradient information leaves only a 0/1 step function. The solver
    cannot smoothly transition between densities, producing blocky transport
    that correlates poorly with the reference caustic's smooth edges.

Contrast stretch alone (H2): Negligible.
    The solver already normalizes energy via boost_ratio = mesh_sum / image_sum.
    Pre-stretching contrast changes the distribution the solver sees, but the
    normalization undoes the global effect. Local contrast is unaffected.

Gamma linearization alone (H1): HURTS.
    Darkens mid-tones, reducing overall contrast for a predominantly-bright
    image like inkbrush. The darker image after linearization gives the solver
    less to work with.

══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
CAVEATS AND NEXT STEPS
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. All absolute edge_r values are low (0.004-0.051). The HYPER pipeline uses
   128px input → ~33k faces. At this resolution, the mesh lacks the geometric
   precision to reproduce fine inkbrush detail. The relative improvements are
   real, but absolute numbers will be higher at NORMAL (512px) resolution.

2. NEXT STEP RECOMMENDED: Confirm H6 improvement at NORMAL (512px) resolution.
   Run: python3 run_pipeline_normal.sh with H6 preprocessed input.
   Expected: absolute edge_r jumps significantly; relative improvement should
   persist or grow.

3. H6 + H1H3 combination not tested. Could be additive: apply Sobel to
   linearized+inverted image. Worth testing if NORMAL-scale results are
   pursued.

4. The Sobel edge map has very low mean brightness (mean=15.8/255 after
   normalization to [0,255]). The solver's boost_ratio will amplify this
   strongly. Verify the OBJ dome height is physically sensible after running
   this preprocessing at production scale.
